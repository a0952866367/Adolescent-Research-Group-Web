Adolescent   Research   Group

We are a group of researchers, students and staff working on the adolescent behavior and health. Our group is interdisciplinary involving clinical care and public health professionals mainly based at National Cheng Kung University with extensive nationwide and international collaborations. Work is going on applications of the existent longitudinal studies as well as investigator-initiated cross-sectional surveys. Research interests include the impact of pubertal timing on adolescent psychosocial health, the interaction between life adversity and social integration on adolescent psychological and behavioral outcomes, and the evaluation of quality of life in children and adolescents with varying chronic conditions.

Projects

  • Impact of pubertal transition on adolescent psychosocial development and health.

  • Social determinants, strain vs. integration, of adolescent health behaviors and psychological outcomes.

  • Development of psychometric measurement tools and application in evaluation of quality of life in children and adolescents.
